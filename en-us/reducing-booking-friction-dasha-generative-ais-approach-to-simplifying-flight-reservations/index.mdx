## Understanding Booking Friction in Flight Reservations

Traditional flight booking methods have several inherent challenges that contribute to booking friction. First and foremost, there is a lack of personalization and contextual understanding. Customers are often expected to input their preferences manually, resulting in a time-consuming and tedious process. Moreover, the overwhelming amount of information presented to customers can be overwhelming and intimidating.

### The Challenges of Traditional Flight Booking

One of the main challenges of traditional flight booking is the extensive amount of data that customers have to sift through. From flight routes to departure times, there are numerous factors to consider. This abundance of information can lead to choice overload, making it difficult for customers to make a decision.

Let's dive deeper into the challenges faced by customers when it comes to traditional flight booking. Imagine you are planning a vacation and want to book a flight. You are presented with a long list of flight options, each with different routes, layovers, and prices. It can be overwhelming to compare all the available choices and select the best one that suits your preferences and budget. This process can be time-consuming and frustrating, especially if you are in a hurry or have specific requirements.

### The Impact of Booking Friction on Customer Experience

The impact of booking friction on customer experience cannot be understated. When customers encounter difficulties during the booking process, they are more likely to abandon the reservation altogether. This not only results in potential revenue loss for airlines, but also creates a negative perception of the airline brand.

Imagine you are trying to book a flight for a business trip, and you encounter multiple roadblocks along the way. The website is slow and unresponsive, making it frustrating to navigate through the different options. Additionally, the booking form requires you to input unnecessary information, causing further frustration and confusion. As a result, you decide to abandon the reservation and look for alternative options. This negative experience not only affects your perception of the airline but also influences your future decisions when it comes to flight bookings.

Booking friction not only impacts individual customers but also has broader implications for the airline industry. When customers face difficulties during the booking process, they are more likely to seek alternative airlines or travel platforms that offer a smoother and more seamless experience. This can lead to a loss of potential revenue and market share for airlines that fail to address booking friction effectively.
