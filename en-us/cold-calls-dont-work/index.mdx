## 🧠 How to Actually Train Dasha to Sound Human

Training your Dasha agent is where the magic happens. It’s not about cramming in more features — it’s about crafting a vibe. Let’s break it down 👇

### 🎭 Define the Role (Make It Real)

Bad:

> “You are a sales agent calling to discuss our solutions for improving lead generation.”

Better:

> “You’re Dasha. You work at a company called ABC that delivers warm leads. You’re calling a business owner for the first time to say hi and see if there's a fit. You’re confident, kind, and chill.”

Your agent needs identity. Voice. Attitude. Otherwise… it’s just another AI blob on the line.

### 🔑 Nail the Opening (Or Lose the Call)

We suggest using **3 pre-recorded lines** that always kick off the call:

1. **The “Hi”**
2. **The “Wait, what’s this about?” question**
3. **The “This is cool, right?” teaser**

**Example:**

> “Hey… yeah, hi. Quick one — you guys do take on new clients, right?”

> “Cool. So, I’m Dasha, and I wanted to hand over like 30 potential leads for your biz — totally free. Just to say hi and let you try us out.”

> “I’ll just ask you a couple things real quick, okay?”

Feels human. Feels real. Feels good.

### ✅ Set a Flexible Plan (Not a Script)

This isn’t theater. This is jazz. Your agent doesn’t need to follow a strict monologue. She needs **a checklist** to flow through, like:

- Get their name
- Ask what industry they’re in
- Understand their current pain points
- Figure out why they haven’t tried something new
- Let them know you’ll set up a test account
- Book a call with a real rep

Think “flight checklist,” not “teleprompter.”

### 💬 Feed It Good Ammo (Knowledge Base)

Dasha’s answers are only as smart as what you give her. Add real facts like:

- “We partner with telecoms to spot buyer intent based on real-world behavior.”
- “Most of our clients see 5–6% conversion with leads that cost less than a dollar.”
- “We only reach out to people who’ve visited specific landing pages — not scraped lists.”

No guesses. No fluff. Just short, punchy answers ready to roll. Your AI as smart as you make it.

### 🗣️ Style Matters: How It Talks

This isn’t about *what* it says. It’s about *how* it says it. Rules:

- Keep it conversational
- Use real pauses, filler words, and soft transitions
- No corporate buzzwords
- No fake urgency

**Example tone:**

> “Sooo yeah — I guess my question is like… do you ever take leads from outside partners or not really?”

Sounds messy? Perfect. That’s the point.

### 📌 Add Ground Rules (But Don’t Go Overboard)

Think of these like bumper lanes in a bowling alley. They keep Dasha on track. Sample rules:

- Always respond to the last thing the person said
- Never back off after the first “no” — get creative
- If the business is under $1M revenue — wrap it up politely
- If the person isn’t the decision-maker — ask for the right contact

That’s it. You don’t need 50 rules. You just need 3–4 good ones.
